Positioning & Value Narrative

780 Potato Patch is the largest single-family home currently on the market in this price tier. At $2,008/sqft, it is priced at a significant discount to competing listings on Forest Road ($2,797–$4,168/sqft) and Beaver Dam ($3,994/sqft) — despite a location that arguably equals or exceeds them for privacy, views, and prestige.

The Vail Mountain Club membership included with the sale carries standalone market value and is not reflected in any competing listing at this price point. The detached guest residence adds income or flexibility that a single-structure comparable cannot match.

The nearest confirmed sold comp (756 Potato Patch, $8M, June 2025) supports the subject's premium — buyers comparing the two will quickly conclude the additional investment is justified by scale, finishes, and amenities. The 2017 Golden Peak sale at $12.6M for a nearly identical bedroom/bath spec, with nearly 2,100 less square footage, further validates current pricing relative to market trajectory.

The Core Problem

The title is written for YouTube click-through rate — emotional, dramatic, curiosity-driven. That approach works for entertainment. For real estate search, it fails. No buyer types "Vail's Architectural Crown Jewel" into Google or asks ChatGPT that phrase.

For GEO (AI search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews), the video description needs to open with clear, structured facts — address, price, specs, agent name, brokerage — so AI engines have something concrete to cite. Without that, this video is invisible to AI-powered search.
